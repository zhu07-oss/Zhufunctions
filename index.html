Transformations

1. Vertical Stretching: In the basic parabolic equation y = -x^2, changing the coefficient of x^2 from -1 to -0.5 results in vertical compression of the graph (compression ratio \frac{1}{2}). Transformation Logic: For the parent equation y = ax², when |a| < 1, the parabola's opening widens (vertical compression). 2. Vertical Translation: Based on the scaled equation y = -0.5x², add 1 to the entire expression, shifting the graph upward by 1 unit..

Domain and Range

Domain: -1 < x < 5 ; Range: (-11.5, 1] .
